Probably the last teaching break as a student? Starting to get busy as thesis submission deadline is approaching and busy with the studies. But anyway, congrats that my paper has been conditionally accepted to the NIME conference! This might be the starting point or maybe not for my academic career path.

Recently I questioned myself a lot about what do HCI really means today, where AI massively grown and what would be the future for interactions between human and AI? Especially when Openclaw gets popular, and security risks rises everywhere. Is it right that we only need language as input?

From a creator perspective, I can see a break between the creative process of human creativity and AI creativity. Human is human because we sense things not only with languages, we listen we see we sense in all kinds of ways. While currently main interactions between human and machines are through prompts which I sense there are potential ways in improving the interactions. Language has it’s own limitation and even one has great control in prompt, they cannot guarantee the machine can output as expected. Human input → AI output recursive production is certainly not the best solution in creativity. I imagine it should be the process of Human input → Human + AI output with interaction designs that bring human into the process of AI creating, especially how to deal with the explosive data that multiple AI agents can provide.
